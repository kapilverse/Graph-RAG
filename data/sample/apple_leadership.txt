Apple Inc. — Leadership and Key People

Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. It was founded on April 1, 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. The company is known for its consumer electronics, software, and online services.

Steve Jobs served as the CEO of Apple from 1997 until his resignation in August 2011 due to health reasons. Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011. Under his leadership, Apple launched revolutionary products including the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad.

Tim Cook succeeded Steve Jobs as CEO of Apple in August 2011. Cook had been Apple's Chief Operating Officer since 2005 and joined the company in 1998 as Senior Vice President of Worldwide Operations. Under Cook's leadership, Apple became the first publicly traded U.S. company to be valued at over $1 trillion, $2 trillion, and $3 trillion.

Craig Federighi is Apple's Senior Vice President of Software Engineering, overseeing the development of iOS, macOS, and other operating systems. He joined Apple in 2009 and reports directly to Tim Cook.

Jeff Williams is Apple's Chief Operating Officer, a role previously held by Tim Cook. Williams oversees Apple's entire supply chain and has been with the company since 1998.

Several notable executives have left Apple to found or lead other companies. Steve Wozniak left Apple in 1985 and founded CL 9, a company that developed the first universal remote control. Later, he founded Wheels of Zeus, a GPS startup that was acquired by Zing in 2006.

Scott Forstall, who was Senior Vice President of iPhone Software at Apple, left the company in 2012 after the Apple Maps controversy. He later worked on various advisory roles in the technology industry.

Tony Fadell, often called the "father of the iPod," was Senior Vice President of the iPod Division at Apple from 2001 to 2008. After leaving Apple, he founded Nest Labs, a home automation company that developed the Nest Learning Thermostat and Nest Protect smoke detector. Nest Labs was acquired by Google (now Alphabet) in 2014 for $3.2 billion.

Andy Rubin, while never directly employed by Apple, co-founded Android Inc. in 2003, which was later acquired by Google. Android became the primary mobile operating system competing with Apple's iOS. Rubin served as Senior Vice President of Android at Google until 2014.
